About Lift

How will you change public contracting to deliver 10x better goods, works or services?

Open Contracting Lift is a new program that will help teams of bold procurement reformers propel their ambitious plans towards systemic change.

Over 14 months, you’ll receive extensive, tailored support from leading experts and peers to help you surmount common barriers to impact and achieve more. Learn how to build buy-in, use open data, and establish better ways to make contracting effective and fair. Then develop and implement your new contracting strategies to deliver better goods, works or services for everyone.

This program is all about achieving your goals. Your goals could be to use contracting to better serve

  • Citizens: building safer roads, making buses run on time, stocking hospitals with the best medicines, or hiring qualified school teachers.
  • Companies: boosting opportunities for women’s, minority owned or local businesses to supply to government, increasing integrity or making your systems more business-friendly.
  • Government officials: making your procurement systems easier to use and more useful to inform decision making, save time and money.

Why Apply?

Propel your reform ambitions.

Get better results from your reform project. You will get a ‘lift’ with intense support to create contracting strategies that help you achieve your ambitious goals.

Learn globally, change locally.

With 100 days of technical support, get bespoke guidance on how to use public contracting to deliver better results. Learn from experts and peers about what works and which mistakes to avoid in building buy-in, using open data, and implementing results driven contracting strategies with vendors and stakeholders.

Join a community of reformers.

Connect with experienced peers who have led successful reforms and overcome similar challenges. Co-work with experts who can help you adapt proven solutions to your context. Join a community of practitioners who are transforming how public contracting works.

Who can apply?

Teams solving problems through contracting.

Lift is open to any teams from the public, private or civil society sectors (or a combination of these sectors) working to improve the quality of a public good, work, or service. You will need to be a team of at least 3 to apply. Teams can work at the national, regional, city, municipality or any levels. The competition is global and open to all* countries.

Plans for real impact from contracting.

Lift is for teams with a plan to achieve measurable impact from their reform project. You are trying to serve your citizens better, engage more diverse vendors or make government officials’ job easier. You have a clear goal in mind, a problem that you are trying to solve.

Projects with momentum, at all levels of maturity.

Teams can be working on projects at different stages of development. You could be embarking on a new initiative, or you could be working to refine and scale a piloted solution. However, reforms need demonstrated support from leadership to qualify.

How does it work?

Teams will be selected through a competitive process. We are looking for all types of groups who have big ideas for using open contracting to improve the quality of public goods, works, or services.

The program has two parts:

Part 1: Building Foundations
Oct 2019 to Jan 2020, 3 months

Five teams learn how to identify strategic entry points, engage stakeholders, apply contracting practices to become more effective, and build robust reform strategies for implementation and monitoring impact.

Part 2: Guiding Implementation
Feb to Dec 2020, 11 months

Three teams are chosen to receive hands-on, custom support as they build, test, and embed best contracting practices in their reform initiatives.

Your commitment

Applicant teams should be prepared to commit to the following:

In the application process

  • Completion of a short application questionnaire for us to learn more about your reform project, team, and context.
  • Up to two rounds of interviews, including a short technical exercise.
  • A round of reference checks.

During the program

  • A 4-day bootcamp to kick off the program; must be available during the week of October 21 or 28.
  • Regular check-in calls with program advisors about every 2 weeks, as well as group seminars on new approaches and 3 in-person workshops throughout the entire program (Part 1 and 2).
  • An estimated 1 day/week of work on your project outside of these engagements, where you’ll be applying feedback or new practices to your work.
